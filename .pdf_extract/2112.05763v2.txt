3.2.4 Trends with stellar evolution variations
There are a few trends visible in the rates in Figure 2. First, the pre-
dicted BHNS rate declines with increasing mass transfer eÔ¨Éciency,
ūĚõĹ (models B, C and D). The increase inūĚõĹ leads the secondary to
accrete more mass during the Ô¨Ārst stable mass transfer phase, re-
sulting in more massive secondaries and typically larger separations
because the mass transfer is more conservative. The larger masses
and/or larger separations at higherūĚõĹ values results in more binaries
eventually disrupting during a SN, merging during the CE phase,
and/or forming a BHBH binary instead of a BHNS (cf., Kruckow
et al. 2018). In addition, this also impactsūĚĎ°inspiral in a complex way
due to the interplay of the typically larger separation after the Ô¨Ārst
stable mass transfer phase, as well as more orbital shrinking during
the CE phase because the star has to eject a more massive envelope.

for ūĚõĹ.ThisdiÔ¨Äerentbehaviorcomesfromanon-trivialcombinationof
waysinwhichtheformationpathwaysofbinariesleadingtomerging
BHNSs are diÔ¨Äerent from merging BHBHs and NSNSs and how
changing ūĚõĹ impacts this. An example is that the impact fromūĚõĹ
on the evolution of the binary is connected with the mass ratios
of the binary (e.g. the mass ratio impacts the size of the Roche
lobes, whether the mass transfer is stable or unstable and whether
the mass transfer causes the binary orbit to widen or shrink). The
merging BHBH and NSNS populations are DCOs with more equal
(i.e. ūĚĎě 1) mass ratio distributions compared to BHNS (see also
¬ß3.4). The binaries that form merging BHBHs and NSNSs originate

stages compared to merging BHNSs. This results in theūĚõĹ parameter
impacting the populations diÔ¨Äerently. Another diÔ¨Äerence is that the
merging BHNS typically form from diÔ¨Äerent formation channels

example, in our Ô¨Āducial model (A) a substantial fraction (& 25% of
all merging BHBHs in our simulation) of the BHBHs form through
only stable mass transfer episodes, without engaging a CE, whereas
for BHNSs this is much smaller (. 5%). The parameter ūĚõĹ has a
